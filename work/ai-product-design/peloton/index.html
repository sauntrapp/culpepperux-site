← AI PRODUCT DESIGN CASE STUDY — WRITING FOR THE B2B BUYER

Pivoting from the athlete to the operator

Show what Peloton usage means for a hospitality partner so they can see whether the amenity is earning its place — and what they need to do next.

A spec case study prepared for Peloton's Commercial Business Unit. Illustrative sample data — not commissioned by or affiliated with Peloton.

00

Where this came from

At KAYAK, my team redesigned the KAYAK for Business onboarding experience. Early on, I noticed the content was written for the executive in seat 2A — the person who'd actually be flying — when the buyer was the corporate travel manager back at the office, comparing tools and signing the contract.

We reframed the messaging around that buyer, and the product started speaking to the person who decides whether to adopt it. The person who takes action.

That pattern isn't a KAYAK quirk. Every "[consumer brand] for Business" inherits its parent's content instincts, which are often tuned to the consumer. This case study explores a scenario of how Peloton could avoid repeating that pattern.

01

The partner and the rider are two different readers

Consumer Peloton product writing speaks to riders in terms of output, cadence and streaks. However, the person running a hotel's Peloton amenity is a GM who thinks in occupancy, guest satisfaction and renewal. The dashboard's job is to translate connected-fitness data into hospitality-business meaning.

02

The dashboard on a healthy month

The hero screen: a hotel operator's Peloton for Business dashboard. It displays a utilization summary with amenity reach and a key action for the operator to renew.

STRATEGIC BRIEF STRATEGIC GOAL

Translate connected-fitness usage into hospitality-business meaning, so an operator sees whether the amenity is earning its place — and what to do next.

KEY MESSAGE + EMOTIONAL DELTA

"Your Peloton amenity is a measurable business asset — it'll tell you plainly when it's working, when it isn't, and what to do." Uncertainty → confident next action.

KEY ACTION

Take the state-appropriate next step — review renewal, get the promo kit, finish activation, or list on Hotel Finder.

KEY METRIC

Right-action rate per state and renewal-conversation rate; directionally, fewer amenity-status support inquiries — not dashboard time-on-page.

05

The pivot back to consumer

The guest's actual entry point: account creation on the equipment touchscreen. This is where the consumer voice briefly returns in the flow.

06

One product, two readers.

The platform and tone each screen uses is a choice about who's reading and what they need to do next.

07

A voice adapting to the audience

Taking a consumer brand's voice to a B2B context is about preserving the spirit while acknowledging the audience. Peloton's voice doesn't change, but its tone does. It's the same product, with content adapted to who's reading and what they need to do next.

The natural next surface would be the guest's post-checkout handoff into the Peloton app. This is the moment the hotel rider becomes a Peloton member.
